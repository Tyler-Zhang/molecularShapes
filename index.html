First, what are molecules?

Molecules are..

- Non-metallic atoms that are covalently bonded together

- They are the smallest unit of a chemical compound that has the ability
to react with other elements

They form when...

- Electrons are shared between atoms so that they both can have a full valence shell

- The pull on electrons have to be similar (difference in EN has to be less than 1.7)

- Different molecules will form different specific shapes according to the way it's bonded

For example

Here we have Chlorine

And Hydrogen

Alone, they have incomplete valence shells

But together... They become a happy molecule with a full valence shell

Why do Molecules Form Specific Shapes?

The VSEPR Theory

- Stands for Valence-shell Electron-Pair Repulsion Theory

- Developed by Gillespie and Nyholm

- We will use this theory to predict molecular shapes

The theory states

Molecules will adjust their shape so that the different valence electron pairs
will be as far away from each other as possible.

- This is due to the negative charges of the electrons repelling each other

For example

Here is a nucleus

And 4 electron pairs

Instead of staying grouped up,

They move themselves to distribute evenly around the nucleus because of repulsion

Lone pairs and Bonds

Bonds and Lone Pairs

- Bonds are where two atoms share electrons

- Lone pairs are pairs of electrons on an atom that isn't shared with other atoms

- They act the same in terms of affecting the molecule's shape

- Bonds are visible in VESPR diagrams where as lone pairs are not

- Lone pairs and bonds only affect the shape when it has a central atom (3 or more in total)

How do we determine a molecule's shape?

Steps to predicting the molecule shape

Lets start by determining the bonds using a Lewis Dot Diagram:

1) Determine the elements in the molecule

2) If there are more than 2 elements, start by placing the element with
the highest bonding capacity in the center

3) Draw the other atoms that are bonded to the central atom

4) Count the amount of bonds and lone pairs in the molecule

5) Draw the Molecule according to the Possible Shapes table

Remember, electron pairs want to be as far as ways as possible. This means that both lone pairs (pairs of electrons not bonded to another atom) and bonded pairs will try to be as far apart to each other as possible.
